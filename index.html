The honest gap — and how I'd close it

I'd be a first-time people manager stepping toward a senior-leadership scope.

I'm not going to pretend the org chart shows 10 years of management. It doesn't. What it shows is a decade of leading without the title — and the technical-leadership credibility that's the hardest part to teach. Here's how I'd de-risk the bet:

  • Lean on a strong second line. I'd over-invest in experienced team leads, listen first, and change the model only after I've earned leader trust.
  • Pair with a mentor at level. I'd ask for an M2/M3 sponsor to accelerate the management-craft I haven't yet practiced formally.
  • Lead with my unfair advantage. No peer brings a working Sales Agent and a shipped agentic app. I'd make AI-led seller productivity the team's signature — and the forecast story.
  • Start where the bar is provable. If the right first step is a first-line SSM team rather than the full Sr Director scope, I'll earn the bigger span by results.
